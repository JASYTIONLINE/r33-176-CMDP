## 1. PURPOSE

### 1.1 Purpose


This SOP establishes policies, responsibilities, and procedures for the execution of driver training, operator qualification, licensing, sustainment training, and vehicle operator management within the 176th EN BDE. It provides standardized guidance for management of the Driver and Operator Standardization Program in accordance with AR 600-55 and applicable Army safety and maintenance regulations to achieve and sustain safe, proficient, and fully qualified operators.


This SOP reinforces operator training, licensing, safety, and maintenance policies established in AR 600-55, AR 385-10, AR 750-1, DA PAM 750-8, TXARNG policy, and applicable state-level guidance. It provides direction for the training, qualification, supervision, and licensing of personnel operating tactical vehicles, engineer equipment, trailers, material handling equipment, ground support equipment, and non-tactical vehicles assigned to subordinate units.


This SOP establishes standardized requirements for operator selection, learner permit management, classroom instruction, hands-on training, PMCS integration, sustainment training, remedial training, licensing validation, and operator records management. Driver training operations will be conducted in accordance with AR 600-55 standards, applicable technical manuals, and equipment-specific training publications.


Training Circulars (TCs), Technical Manuals (TMs), Field Manuals (FMs), Army Regulations (ARs), Department of the Army Pamphlets (DA PAMs), and other applicable publications used to support operator training will be obtained from the Army Publishing Directorate (APD) website located at https://armypubs.army.mil unless otherwise directed by higher headquarters.


Units conducting operator training will maintain access to current applicable publications required to support operator training, testing, licensing, sustainment training, and evaluation operations.


Operator training, licensing, records, and sustainment requirements are subject to validation and enforcement through the Command Maintenance Discipline Program (CMDP). Commanders at all levels will ensure operator training is conducted to standard, properly documented, supervised, risk managed, and continuously assessed for compliance, safety, proficiency, accident prevention, and readiness.

---

## 2. APPLICABILITY

This SOP applies to all units and personnel assigned or attached to the 176th EN BDE who operate, supervise, train, test, evaluate, dispatch, or manage Army vehicles or equipment requiring operator qualification or licensing. Personnel operating vehicles or equipment must meet all training, qualification, supervision, and licensing requirements in accordance with applicable Army regulations and unit SOPs.

Supplementation of this SOP is authorized at the unit level. Subordinate unit SOPs will not conflict with this SOP or higher-level regulations and will be approved by the unit commander.

## 4. ORGANIZATION FOR DRIVER TRAINING AND LICENSING OPERATIONS

### 4.1 General


Driver training and operator licensing are continuous processes conducted in both garrison and field environments and are command responsibilities at all levels. Driver training operations will be planned, scheduled, supervised, documented, and evaluated to ensure operators are properly trained, licensed, and proficient in the safe operation of assigned vehicles and equipment.


Driver training requirements will be integrated into unit training plans and reflected on training schedules. Units will allocate sufficient time and resources to conduct operator training, sustainment training, testing, and licensing operations in accordance with AR 600-55 and applicable Army regulations.


The chain of command exercises control over driver training and licensing activities. Commanders will establish a driver training structure that clearly defines responsibilities and ensures effective execution of operator training, testing, licensing, sustainment, supervision, and records management operations. Driver training organization and requirements are based on unit MTOE, assigned equipment, mission requirements, and applicable Army and TXARNG policies.


Driver training managers and responsibilities are defined in section 6 and are responsible for planning, organizing, directing, coordinating, supervising, documenting, and evaluating operator training and licensing operations.


### 4.2 Driver Training Structure


The Driver and Operator Standardization Program consists of structured operator training, testing, qualification, licensing, sustainment, and supervision operations conducted in accordance with AR 600-55.


Driver training operations consist of:


Initial operator training


Equipment-specific training


Variant-specific qualification when required


Learner permit operations


PMCS instruction and validation


Written and performance testing


Sustainment and remedial training


Night Vision Device (NVD) qualification and sustainment training when applicable


Field and road driving operations


Ground guide training


The Driver and Operator Standardization Program is executed at the unit level through qualified personnel appointed in writing and supervised through the chain of command.


### 4.3 Driver Training Operations Framework


Driver training operations within the brigade will be executed through a structured framework consisting of:


Operator selection and screening


Classroom instruction


Hands-on operator training


Road and field driving operations


PMCS instruction and validation


Ground guide training


Operator testing and evaluation


Licensing and qualification validation


Sustainment and remedial training


NVD qualification and sustainment training when applicable


Driver records management


Risk management and safety integration


All operator training, testing, licensing, sustainment, and qualification actions will be documented and maintained in accordance with AR 600-55 and applicable records management requirements.


### 4.4 Driver Training Support Relationships


Battalions and companies may receive support for operator training and licensing operations from designated organizations and agencies including:


Field Maintenance Shops (FMS)


Unit Training Equipment Sites (UTES)


Maneuver Area Training Equipment Sites (MATES)


Forward Support Companies (FSC)


Field Maintenance Teams (FMT)


Installation Driver Testing and Licensing Offices


Installation Safety Offices


Supporting maintenance activities


Units will coordinate with assigned and supporting organizations to ensure availability of training areas, training equipment, maintenance support, recovery support, and technical assistance required to conduct operator training safely and effectively.


### 4.5 Driver Training Planning and Integration


Driver training and sustainment operations will be integrated into all training and operational planning.


Driver training periods will be scheduled on unit training calendars.


Commanders will ensure PMCS instruction and validation are integrated into operator training events.


Operator licensing priorities will be aligned with assigned equipment, mission requirements, and MTOE requirements.


Resources will be allocated to support driver training, sustainment training, licensing operations, and testing requirements.


Driver training planning will ensure operators are properly trained, supervised, licensed, and proficient to support operational requirements.


### 4.6 Driver Training Responsibility and Accountability


Driver training and operator licensing are command responsibilities conducted through the chain of command and supervised at all levels.


All operator training, licensing, sustainment, testing, and qualification actions will be properly documented and maintained in accordance with AR 600-55 and applicable records management requirements.


Operator qualification records, training documentation, learner permits, licensing packets, and sustainment records are subject to inspection and validation through CMDP and other command inspection programs.


Operators will not operate Army vehicles or equipment without proper qualification, supervision, licensing, and authorization.

## 5. DRIVER AND OPERATOR STANDARDIZATION PROGRAM

### 5.1 General


The Driver and Operator Standardization Program will be conducted in accordance with AR 600-55 and applicable Army regulations, technical manuals, training circulars, installation policies, and TXARNG guidance.


The Driver and Operator Standardization Program establishes standardized requirements for the selection, training, testing, qualification, licensing, supervision, sustainment, and evaluation of Army vehicle and equipment operators.


Driver training and licensing programs will be managed at the unit level under command supervision and integrated into maintenance, safety, and readiness programs.


Operators will not operate Army vehicles or equipment without proper qualification, licensing, supervision, and authorization.


### 5.2 Program Requirements


The Driver and Operator Standardization Program will include:


Operator selection and screening


Classroom instruction


Hands-on operator training


Road and field driving operations


PMCS instruction and validation


Ground guide training


Written and performance testing


Equipment-specific qualification


Variant-specific qualification when applicable


Learner permit operations


Licensing and qualification validation


Sustainment and refresher training


Remedial training


Night Vision Device (NVD) qualification and sustainment training when applicable


Driver records management


Risk management and safety integration


All operator training will be conducted using applicable technical manuals, training circulars, field manuals, Army regulations, and approved Army publications applicable to the equipment being operated.


### 5.3 Operator Qualification Requirements


Operators will:


Be medically qualified to operate assigned equipment


Possess a valid civilian driver’s license when required


Successfully complete required classroom and hands-on training


Successfully complete required written and performance testing


Demonstrate proficiency on the specific equipment and variant operated


Complete PMCS training and validation


Comply with sustainment and refresher training requirements


Meet installation and host nation licensing requirements when applicable


Operators will not operate Army vehicles or equipment outside the scope of their qualification and authorization.


### 5.4 Equipment Qualification and Variant Requirements


Operators will be trained, tested, and licensed on the specific equipment platform and variant operated.


Qualification on one vehicle or equipment variant does not automatically authorize operation of other variants unless specifically authorized by regulation, technical guidance, or command policy.


Units will ensure operators receive familiarization and qualification training on:

- Assigned vehicle variants

- Trailer configurations

- Additional equipment systems

- Mission-specific equipment configurations


Variant qualification requirements will be documented in operator training and licensing records.


### 5.5 Learner Permit Program


Learner permits will be issued and managed in accordance with AR 600-55 and section 7 of this SOP.


Learner permit operations, supervision requirements, restrictions, and documentation requirements are established in section 7 and section 8 of this SOP.


### 5.6 Licensing Program


Operator licensing procedures will be conducted in accordance with AR 600-55 and section 7 of this SOP.


Licensing procedures, qualification requirements, sustainment requirements, restrictions, documentation standards, and operator authorization requirements are established in section 7, section 9, and section 10 of this SOP.


### 5.7 Sustainment Training Program


Units will conduct sustainment training to maintain operator proficiency, safety awareness, PMCS standards, and regulatory compliance in accordance with AR 600-55 and section 8 of this SOP.


Detailed sustainment training requirements, reevaluation requirements, refresher training requirements, and documentation procedures are established in section 8 and section 10 of this SOP.


### 5.8 Remedial Training Program


Remedial training will be conducted following accidents, unsafe acts, licensing violations, PMCS deficiencies, operator negligence, command-directed corrective actions, or demonstrated operator deficiencies.


Detailed remedial training procedures, reevaluation requirements, corrective actions, and documentation requirements are established in section 7, section 8, section 10, and section 12 of this SOP.


### 5.9 NVD Qualification Program


Units conducting NVD operations will establish NVD qualification and sustainment programs in accordance with AR 600-55, TC 21-305-2, applicable TC 21-305 series publications, and section 8 and section 9 of this SOP.


Detailed NVD training requirements, qualification requirements, sustainment requirements, evaluation procedures, and records management requirements are established in section 7, section 8, section 9, and section 10 of this SOP.


### 5.10 Driver Packet Requirements


Units will maintain operator packets in accordance with AR 600-55, DA PAM 750-8, section 10 of this SOP, and applicable CMDP requirements.


Operator packets will remain current, accurate, complete, standardized, and available for inspection.


### 5.11 Licensing Validation and CMDP Integration


Operator training, qualification, licensing, sustainment, records management, and packet administration operations are subject to inspection and validation through CMDP and command inspection programs.


Detailed CMDP oversight requirements, inspection procedures, corrective action requirements, QC/QA procedures, and compliance enforcement procedures are established in section 12 of this SOP.

## 6. RESPONSIBILITIES

### 6.1 Brigade Commander


The Brigade Commander is responsible for the overall execution, oversight, standardization, and enforcement of the brigade Driver and Operator Standardization Program.


The Brigade Commander will:


- Establish brigade driver training and licensing policy in accordance with AR 600-55, applicable Army regulations, and TXARNG guidance

- Ensure subordinate battalions maintain effective Driver and Operator Standardization Programs

- Ensure subordinate driver training and operator licensing programs support readiness, safety, maintenance discipline, and mission requirements

- Allocate sufficient resources to support brigade driver training and licensing operations

- Enforce compliance with operator training, sustainment, licensing, and records management requirements

- Ensure CMDP inspections include evaluation of driver training and licensing operations

- Designate brigade personnel responsible for oversight and standardization of operator training and licensing operations

- Ensure subordinate commanders maintain accountability for execution of driver training and licensing requirements

- Review systemic operator training, licensing, sustainment, accident, and readiness deficiencies affecting brigade operations

- Direct corrective actions for deficiencies identified through inspections, evaluations, audits, accident trends, or command reviews

- Ensure subordinate organizations maintain driver training programs nested with brigade policy and applicable Army regulations


### 6.2 Brigade Executive Officer


The Brigade Executive Officer is responsible for supervising and synchronizing brigade driver training and operator licensing readiness across subordinate battalions.


The Brigade Executive Officer serves as the Brigade Commander’s primary staff officer for oversight of subordinate battalion driver training program execution, accountability, and compliance.


The Brigade Executive Officer works in coordination with the Brigade Maintenance Supervisor to ensure compliance, standardization, quality assurance (QA), and quality control (QC) of the brigade Driver and Operator Standardization Program.


The Brigade Executive Officer will:


- Supervise brigade driver training and operator licensing readiness programs on behalf of the Brigade Commander

- Ensure subordinate battalion executive officers maintain accountability and oversight of battalion Driver and Operator Standardization Programs

- Synchronize driver training, sustainment training, licensing, and operator readiness efforts across subordinate battalions

- Monitor brigade-level trends affecting operator readiness, licensing compliance, sustainment training, accident prevention, and CMDP performance

- Ensure operator training and licensing deficiencies identified during inspections, evaluations, audits, or command reviews are corrected

- Monitor subordinate battalion compliance with brigade policy, AR 600-55, CMDP requirements, and applicable Army regulations

- Ensure driver training and licensing requirements are integrated into brigade maintenance and readiness programs

- Coordinate with the Brigade Maintenance Supervisor and Battalion Executive Officers regarding operator readiness and licensing issues affecting brigade operations

- Assist the Brigade Commander with enforcement of operator training and licensing standards throughout the brigade

- Monitor effectiveness of subordinate battalion Driver and Operator Standardization Programs and direct corrective actions when required

- Assist with brigade-level QA/QC oversight of subordinate battalion operator training, licensing, sustainment, and records management programs


### 6.3 Brigade Maintenance Supervisor


The Brigade Maintenance Supervisor serves as the Brigade Commander’s senior enlisted advisor for maintenance-related driver training standardization, operator licensing oversight, CMDP integration, and sustainment program compliance across subordinate battalions.


The Brigade Maintenance Supervisor works in coordination with the Brigade Executive Officer to ensure compliance, standardization, quality assurance (QA), and quality control (QC) of the brigade Driver and Operator Standardization Program.


The Brigade Maintenance Supervisor will:


- Advise brigade leadership on driver training, operator licensing, sustainment training, CMDP trends, and operator readiness issues affecting brigade operations

- Ensure Battalion Master Drivers are properly trained and qualified to manage battalion Driver and Operator Standardization Programs

- Assist with standardization of driver training and licensing operations throughout subordinate battalions

- Monitor battalion driver training and licensing programs for compliance with brigade policy, AR 600-55, CMDP requirements, and applicable Army regulations

- Conduct spot checks, assistance visits, and CMDP inspections of operator training and licensing programs

- Validate operator training, sustainment training, licensing operations, and records management are conducted to standard

- Monitor HHC and subordinate battalion operator records for accuracy, completeness, and regulatory compliance

- Identify systemic deficiencies and trends affecting operator readiness, sustainment training, licensing compliance, and CMDP performance

- Assist subordinate organizations with corrective actions related to operator training and licensing deficiencies

- Verify subordinate organizations maintain access to current Army publications, technical manuals, training circulars, and official Army driver training resources required to support operator training operations

- Assist with preparation for inspections, evaluations, audits, and readiness reviews related to operator training and licensing operations

- Coordinate with Battalion Master Drivers regarding operator training, licensing, sustainment, inspection, and standardization issues affecting subordinate battalion programs


The Brigade Maintenance Supervisor provides brigade-level oversight, standardization, inspection, synchronization, and compliance verification of subordinate driver training and licensing programs through the CMDP inspection process.


### 6.4 Battalion Commanders


The Battalion Commander is responsible for execution, oversight, enforcement, and effectiveness of the battalion Driver and Operator Standardization Program.


The Battalion Commander will:


- Establish and maintain a battalion Driver and Operator Standardization Program in accordance with brigade policy, AR 600-55, CMDP requirements, and applicable Army regulations

- Ensure subordinate companies maintain compliant operator training and licensing programs

- Ensure operators are properly trained, tested, supervised, licensed, and qualified prior to operating Army vehicles or equipment

- Ensure driver training and sustainment requirements are integrated into battalion training plans and readiness operations

- Ensure operator training, sustainment, and licensing requirements support mission readiness and safe equipment operation

- Approve operator training areas and routes in accordance with local risk management requirements

- Ensure operator training areas and routes are periodically reviewed for safety, suitability, and effectiveness

- Appoint in writing:

  - Battalion Master Driver

  - Additional personnel required by regulation or command policy

- Ensure CMDP inspections include evaluation of operator training, licensing, sustainment, and records management operations

- Ensure deficiencies identified during inspections, evaluations, audits, or command reviews are corrected

- Ensure battalion operators maintain current and valid licenses for assigned equipment

- Ensure sustainment and remedial training programs are conducted and documented

- Ensure subordinate organizations maintain current applicable Army publications, technical manuals, training circulars, and training references required to support operator training operations

- Enforce compliance with brigade driver training policy and applicable Army regulations

- Maintain oversight of operator readiness, licensing status, sustainment training compliance, and driver training program effectiveness throughout the battalion


### 6.5 Battalion Executive Officers


The Battalion Executive Officer is responsible for supervising, synchronizing, and enforcing execution of the battalion Driver and Operator Standardization Program on behalf of the Battalion Commander.


The Battalion Executive Officer serves as the Battalion Commander’s primary staff officer for oversight of battalion operator training, licensing, sustainment, inspection, and compliance operations.


The Battalion Executive Officer works in coordination with the Brigade Executive Officer and Battalion Master Driver to ensure compliance, standardization, accountability, and effectiveness of the battalion Driver and Operator Standardization Program.


The Battalion Executive Officer will:


- Supervise battalion driver training and operator licensing readiness programs

- Ensure subordinate company executive officers maintain accountability and oversight of company driver training and licensing operations

- Synchronize operator training, sustainment training, licensing, and readiness requirements throughout the battalion

- Monitor battalion trends affecting operator readiness, licensing compliance, sustainment training, accident prevention, and CMDP performance

- Assist company leadership with resolution of operator training and licensing deficiencies

- Ensure company-level operator training programs remain nested with battalion and brigade policy

- Assist with battalion-level QA/QC oversight of operator training, licensing, sustainment, and records management operations

- Enforce compliance with battalion driver training policy, CMDP requirements, and applicable Army regulations

The Battalion Master Driver serves as the Battalion Commander’s principal advisor and program manager for driver training, operator standardization, licensing, sustainment training oversight, and operator records management.

The Battalion Master Driver is responsible for oversight, standardization, inspection, validation, and compliance enforcement of the battalion Driver and Operator Standardization Program.


The Battalion Master Driver will:


- Advise the Battalion Commander on driver training, licensing, sustainment, and operator readiness matters

- Establish battalion standards for operator packet management, sustainment training, licensing operations, and documentation

- Supervise and standardize driver training and licensing operations throughout subordinate units

- Conduct quality control (QC) and quality assurance (QA) of operator packets throughout the battalion

- Ensure operator records and packet management operations comply with section 10 of this SOP

- Validate operator licensing documentation and packet accuracy through CMDP inspections and command reviews

- Monitor sustainment and refresher training compliance throughout the battalion

- Assist subordinate units with corrective actions related to operator training and licensing deficiencies

- Monitor licensing expiration dates, sustainment requirements, and operator qualification status

- Coordinate battalion operator training requirements with subordinate units and battalion staff

- Maintain battalion-level oversight of operator records, packet management, licensing tracking, sustainment tracking, qualification status, and licensing validation operations in accordance with section 10 of this SOP

  - Army Publishing Directorate (APD): https://armypubs.army.mil

- Assist with preparation for inspections, evaluations, audits, and readiness reviews related to operator training and licensing operations


The Battalion Master Driver is responsible for program oversight, standardization, inspection, validation, and compliance enforcement. Subordinate unit personnel responsible for day-to-day packet maintenance, sustainment tracking, and operator documentation management will maintain and service operator records at the unit and platoon level.

### 6.12 License Instructors


License Instructors are commander-appointed personnel responsible for conducting operator training and assisting with execution of the unit Driver and Operator Standardization Program.


License Instructors will conduct operator training in accordance with AR 600-55, applicable Army regulations, technical manuals, training circulars, equipment publications, and unit SOPs.


License Instructors work directly with Unit Operator Training Representatives, Platoon Operator Training Representatives, License Examiners, and unit leadership to conduct and standardize operator training operations.


License Instructors hold the rank of Sergeant (E5) or above and possess the experience, maturity, technical knowledge, and communication skills necessary to effectively train and supervise operators.


License Instructors will:


- Conduct classroom and hands-on operator training

- Train operators on PMCS procedures, safety requirements, emergency procedures, equipment limitations, vehicle operation, ground guide procedures, accident prevention, recovery awareness, and NVD operations when applicable

- Utilize applicable equipment-specific training publications, technical manuals, training circulars, and Army instructional materials while conducting operator training

- Maintain familiarity with current Army driver training standards, regulations, technical manuals, training circulars, and instructional guidance applicable to equipment operated within the unit

- Periodically review official Army training resources, regulations, technical publications, and instructional materials to ensure operator training is conducted using the most current approved standards and guidance

- Ensure operators demonstrate proficiency prior to advancement to testing

- Supervise learner permit operators during training operations

- Ensure training vehicles are inspected prior to use

- Ensure required PPE is utilized during training operations

- Document completed training and attendance rosters


### 6.13 License Examiners


License Examiners are commander-appointed personnel responsible for conducting operator testing, evaluations, and qualification validation in support of the unit Driver and Operator Standardization Program.


License Examiners hold the rank of Staff Sergeant (E6) or above and possess the experience, maturity, technical knowledge, and judgment necessary to evaluate operator proficiency and enforce operator training standards.


License Examiners will conduct testing and evaluations in accordance with AR 600-55, applicable Army regulations, technical manuals, training circulars, equipment publications, and unit SOPs.


License Examiners work directly with Unit Operator Training Representatives, Platoon Operator Training Representatives, License Instructors, and unit leadership to standardize and validate operator qualification operations.


License Examiners will:


- Conduct written and performance testing in accordance with AR 600-55

- Verify operators demonstrate proficiency on the specific equipment and variant for which licensing is requested

- Conduct PMCS evaluations during operator qualification testing

- Utilize applicable equipment-specific training publications, technical manuals, training circulars, and Army evaluation guidance while conducting operator testing and evaluations

- Maintain familiarity with current Army driver training standards, regulations, technical manuals, training circulars, and evaluation guidance applicable to equipment operated within the unit

- Periodically review official Army training resources, regulations, technical publications, and instructional materials to ensure testing and evaluations are conducted using the most current approved standards and guidance

- Ensure testing standards are applied consistently

- Ensure operators achieve the minimum passing score established by regulation or command policy

- Document testing results on required forms and records

- Recommend licensing approval, retraining, or disqualification actions based on operator performance

- License Examiners will not test or evaluate personnel they personally trained. When a License Examiner conducts operator training as a License Instructor, operator testing and evaluation will be conducted by a different qualified and appointed License Examiner.


### 6.14 Platoon Leaders and Platoon Sergeants


Platoon Leaders and Platoon Sergeants are responsible for supervision, enforcement, accountability, and execution of operator training, sustainment, licensing compliance, and operator readiness requirements within the platoon.


Platoon Leaders and Platoon Sergeants will:


- Ensure platoon operators are properly trained, supervised, licensed, and qualified prior to operating Army vehicles or equipment

- Allocate sufficient time, personnel, equipment, scheduling support, and logistical support to enable platoon operator training and licensing personnel to execute assigned duties and mission requirements

- Ensure platoon operators maintain current licenses and qualifications for assigned equipment and variants

- Select, appoint, mentor, train, supervise, and validate personnel assigned operator training and licensing duties within the platoon

- Ensure assigned operator training and licensing personnel are performing duties to standard

- Ensure sustainment and refresher training requirements are completed and documented

- Coordinate operator training requirements and deficiencies with company leadership and Unit Operator Training Representatives

- Enforce operator training standards, safety requirements, and licensing compliance within the platoon

- Maintain visibility of operator readiness, sustainment status, licensing deficiencies, and training requirements affecting platoon readiness


### 6.15 Section Leaders and First-Line Supervisors


Section Leaders and First-Line Supervisors are the final supervisory level responsible for operator qualification oversight, licensing compliance, sustainment accountability, and operator readiness within their assigned section.


Section Leaders and First-Line Supervisors will:


- Maintain continuous visibility of operator qualification, licensing, sustainment training, and readiness status within the section

- Ensure unqualified, unlicensed, expired, suspended, revoked, or deficient personnel are not assigned to operate Army vehicles or equipment

- Identify and report operator training, licensing, sustainment, PMCS, and readiness deficiencies through the chain of command

- Ensure corrective actions are initiated for identified operator deficiencies affecting readiness or compliance

- Coordinate operator training, sustainment, retraining, and licensing requirements through platoon leadership and operator training representatives

- Enforce operator training standards, PMCS standards, safety requirements, and licensing compliance within the section


### 6.16 Operators


Operators are responsible for maintaining proficiency, complying with operator training and licensing requirements, performing assigned duties to standard, and operating Army vehicles and equipment safely and within the limits of their training and qualification.


Soldiers are professional operators, not just drivers. At all times operators will exercise personal responsibility for their own safety and the safety of other personnel during vehicle and equipment operations and immediately report unsafe conditions, unsafe acts, accidents, or hazards through the chain of command.


Operators will:


- Operate only equipment for which they are properly trained, licensed, qualified, and authorized

- Maintain awareness of their operator qualification, licensing, sustainment, and medical status

- Immediately notify leadership of changes affecting their qualification, licensing status, medical readiness, or ability to safely operate assigned equipment

- Report expired, suspended, revoked, deficient, or missing operator documentation to leadership no later than the next scheduled drill or duty day

- Inform leadership when additional training, sustainment training, retraining, or qualification is required

- Perform PMCS and operate equipment in accordance with applicable Army regulations, technical manuals, safety requirements, and unit SOPs

- Report equipment faults, accidents, unsafe conditions, and operator deficiencies through the chain of command

## 7. DRIVER QUALIFICATION AND LICENSING REQUIREMENTS

### 7.1 General


Operator qualification and licensing operations will be conducted in accordance with AR 600-55, applicable Army regulations, technical manuals, training circulars, installation policies, and brigade policy.


Operators will not operate Army vehicles or equipment unless properly trained, tested, licensed, qualified, and authorized.


Operator qualification, licensing, sustainment, and records management operations are subject to inspection and validation through CMDP and command inspection programs.

TXARNG personnel will comply with applicable Army National Guard operator training, sustainment, reevaluation, and licensing requirements when those requirements differ from Active Component standards.


### 7.2 Selection Requirements


Driver applicants will:


- Meet medical qualification requirements in accordance with applicable Army regulations

- Possess a valid civilian driver’s license when required

- Demonstrate maturity, reliability, and sound judgment

- Be capable of understanding vehicle operation, PMCS procedures, safety requirements, and operator responsibilities

- Complete required administrative documentation and qualification records

- Successfully complete required academic, written, and hands-on training requirements

- Successfully complete required operator testing and evaluation requirements

- Meet local installation, theater, and command requirements when applicable


### 7.3 Disqualifying Conditions


Personnel will not operate Army vehicles or equipment when:


- Medically restricted from vehicle or equipment operation

- Under the influence of alcohol, illegal drugs, controlled substances, or medications affecting safe operation

- License privileges are suspended, revoked, or expired

- Required training, sustainment training, testing, or qualification requirements are incomplete

- Required supervision is unavailable for learner permit operations

- Demonstrated unsafe operation, negligence, or reckless behavior

- Involved in unresolved licensing, safety, medical, or command actions affecting operator qualification or operation eligibility


### 7.4 Learner Permit Requirements


Learner permits will be issued in accordance with AR 600-55.


Personnel operating under a learner permit will:


- Operate only equipment authorized on the learner permit

- Be supervised by a qualified and licensed operator, License Instructor, or designated trainer

- Operate only during approved training operations

- Comply with all supervision restrictions and training limitations


Learner permit holders will not independently operate Army vehicles or equipment.


### 7.5 Licensing Procedures


Operators will successfully complete required training, testing, and evaluation requirements prior to issuance of operator licenses.


Licensing procedures will include:


- Administrative screening

- Medical qualification verification

- Classroom instruction

- Hands-on operator training

- PMCS instruction and validation

- Written testing

- Performance testing

- Equipment-specific qualification

- Sustainment training when applicable

- Documentation and records validation


Operators will only be licensed on equipment and variants for which they have been properly trained and tested.


### 7.6 Operator Licenses


Qualified operators may be issued:


- OF 346

- DA Form 5984-E

- Additional qualification documentation as required


Licenses will:


- Identify authorized equipment and variants

- Include restrictions when applicable

- Remain current and legible

- Be maintained in operator records


Operators will not operate equipment outside the scope of their license or authorization.


### 7.7 Equipment Qualification Requirements


Operators will be trained, tested, and licensed on the specific equipment platform and variant operated.


Qualification on one platform or variant does not automatically authorize operation of other platforms or variants unless specifically authorized by regulation or command policy.


Units will ensure operators receive qualification and familiarization training on assigned equipment configurations, trailers, and mission-specific systems.


### 7.8 Sustainment Training Requirements


Units will conduct sustainment training to maintain operator proficiency, licensing compliance, PMCS standards, and safety awareness in accordance with AR 600-55 and section 8 of this SOP.


Detailed sustainment training requirements, check ride requirements, reevaluation procedures, and sustainment documentation requirements are established in section 8 and section 10 of this SOP.


### 7.9 Remedial Training Requirements


Remedial training will be conducted following:


- Accidents

- Unsafe acts

- Licensing violations

- PMCS deficiencies

- Operator negligence

- Command-directed corrective action

- Demonstrated operator deficiencies


Remedial training may require retraining, reevaluation, or requalification prior to restoration of operator privileges. The scope and severity of corrective actions will be determined by the chain of command based on the nature of the deficiency, unsafe act, accident, or violation.


### 7.10 License Suspension and Revocation


Commanders may suspend or revoke operator privileges for:


- Unsafe operation

- Negligence

- Reckless behavior

- Alcohol or drug involvement

- Failure to maintain qualification requirements

- Medical disqualification

- Failure to comply with operator training or safety requirements

- Command-directed safety actions


Suspension or revocation actions will be documented and maintained in operator records.


### 7.11 NVD Qualification Requirements


Operators conducting NVD operations will successfully complete required NVD qualification and sustainment training in accordance with AR 600-55, TC 21-305-2, applicable TC 21-305 series publications, and section 8 and section 9 of this SOP.

### 7.12 Driver Packet Requirements


Units will maintain operator packets in accordance with AR 600-55, DA PAM 750-8, applicable CMDP requirements, and section 10 of this SOP.

## 8. DRIVER TRAINING PROGRAM

### 8.1 General


Driver training programs will be conducted in accordance with AR 600-55, AR 385-10, applicable training circulars (TCs), applicable technical manuals (TMs), installation requirements, applicable TXARNG guidance, and this SOP.


Units conducting operator training will maintain access to current Army publications, technical manuals, training circulars, training support packages, and official Army driver training resources required to support operator training operations.


Driver training programs will be:

- Progressive

- Performance-oriented

- Risk managed

- Equipment-specific

- Variant-specific when applicable


Training programs will integrate:

- Safety

- PMCS

- Risk management

- Ground guiding

- Accident prevention

- Environmental considerations

- Tactical operations when applicable


### 8.2 Training Phases


Driver training will include:

- Phase I – Initial operator training

- Phase II – Equipment-specific training

- Phase III – Validation, testing, and licensing


Training progression will move from:

- Classroom instruction

- Basic familiarization

- Hands-on operation

- Road and field operation

- Tactical operation when applicable

- Validation and testing


Training will be conducted using applicable equipment-specific technical manuals, training circulars, and official Army instructional materials.


### 8.3 Classroom Training


Classroom instruction will be conducted in accordance with AR 600-55, AR 385-10, TC 21-305 series publications for wheeled vehicle operations, TC 21-306 for tracked vehicle operations when applicable, applicable equipment technical manuals (TMs), Army-developed training support packages (TSPs), and this SOP.


Training circulars (TCs) and associated Army-developed training support packages (TSPs) applicable to the equipment platform being operated establish required instructional tasks, academic requirements, evaluation criteria, performance standards, and training progression requirements for that equipment.


Classroom instruction will address operator responsibilities, safety requirements, PMCS standards, emergency procedures, dispatch awareness, recovery awareness, accident prevention, and equipment-specific operational requirements applicable to the equipment being operated.


Academic training completion will be documented within the operator training packet in accordance with AR 600-55.


### 8.4 Hands-On Training


Hands-on training will be conducted by qualified personnel using applicable Army regulations, technical manuals, training circulars, equipment training publications, approved training areas, and risk-managed training routes.


Training will be progressive and performance-oriented and will be conducted using the equipment platform and variant for which qualification is required.


Training circulars (TCs) applicable to the equipment platform being operated establish the required operator training tasks, performance standards, instructional requirements, evaluation criteria, and progression requirements for that equipment.


Training routes and training areas will be approved by commanders and risk assessed prior to use.


### 8.5 PMCS Training


Preventive Maintenance Checks and Services (PMCS) is a continuous operator-level maintenance process conducted to identify deficiencies, maintain equipment readiness, prevent equipment failure, and ensure safe operation in accordance with AR 750-1, DA PAM 750-8, applicable technical manuals, lubrication orders, and maintenance SOP requirements.


PMCS consists of preventive maintenance, checks, and services.


Preventive maintenance includes routine operator actions required to preserve equipment condition and readiness, including cleaning, lubrication, battery maintenance, fluid servicing, tire maintenance, corrosion prevention, and care of associated equipment and components as prescribed by applicable technical manuals and maintenance publications.


Checks consist of inspections and operational evaluations conducted using PMCS tables, technical manuals, and operator procedures to identify faults, deficiencies, safety hazards, and deadlining conditions.


Services consist of scheduled operator-level and maintenance-level service actions conducted in accordance with technical manuals, maintenance regulations, lubrication orders, and service schedules.


PMCS training is not limited to completion of PMCS tables contained within technical manuals. Operators are responsible for understanding equipment capabilities, limitations, operational requirements, safety requirements, emergency procedures, fault identification and reporting procedures, deadlining conditions, operator-level maintenance responsibilities, and proper operation of assigned systems, mission equipment, and auxiliary equipment applicable to assigned platforms.


Operators will receive PMCS training appropriate to assigned equipment and mission requirements, including operation under usual and unusual conditions, recovery awareness, and operation of mission systems such as load handling systems, winches, trailers, and associated equipment when applicable.


Operators will demonstrate PMCS proficiency prior to recommendation for operator qualification or licensing.


### 8.6 Learner Permits


Learner permits will be issued in accordance with AR 600-55.


Personnel operating under a learner permit will:


- Operate only equipment authorized on the learner permit

- Be supervised by a qualified and licensed operator, License Instructor, or designated trainer

- Operate only during approved training operations

- Comply with all supervision restrictions and training limitations


Learner permit holders will not independently operate Army vehicles or equipment.


### 8.7 Ground Guide Training


Ground guide training will be conducted in accordance with AR 385-10, TC 3-21.60, applicable technical manuals, and this SOP.


Ground guide training will ensure operators and ground guides understand required hand and arm signals, communication procedures, positioning requirements, blind spot hazards, backing procedures, night operations requirements, and emergency stop procedures applicable to military vehicle operations.


Ground guide procedures will be enforced during vehicle operations in accordance with Army safety requirements and unit SOPs.


### 8.8 Convoy Training


Units conducting convoy operations will maintain a convoy training program appropriate to assigned mission requirements and operational environments.


Convoy training will be conducted in accordance with ATP 4-01.45 Tactical Convoy Operations, AR 600-55, AR 385-10, applicable technical manuals, risk management requirements, and this SOP.


Operators participating in convoy operations will receive training on convoy procedures, vehicle intervals, movement techniques, communication procedures, convoy safety requirements, accident prevention, emergency procedures, recovery awareness, and duties applicable to assigned convoy positions and mission requirements.


Convoy operations will comply with applicable dispatch, safety, mission planning, and risk management requirements.


### 8.9 Night and Limited Visibility Training


Units with NVD-equipped vehicles or NVD devices assigned to their inventory will maintain an active night and limited visibility training and licensing program.


Personnel will not conduct NVD operations until properly trained, qualified, evaluated, and licensed in accordance with AR 600-55, AR 385-10, TC 21-305-2, applicable TC 21-305 series publications, applicable technical manuals, and this SOP.


Units lacking qualified instructors, examiners, or trainers for NVD operations will coordinate with supporting organizations, training support agencies, installation resources, or other qualified units to obtain required training support and develop internal train-the-trainer capability.


Night and limited visibility training will be conducted using TC 21-305-2, applicable TC 21-305 series publications, applicable technical manuals, ATP 5-19, and unit NVD SOP requirements.


NVD qualification and sustainment records will be maintained within operator packets in accordance with AR 600-55.


### 8.10 Recovery Awareness Training


Operators will receive self-recovery awareness training appropriate to assigned equipment and mission requirements in accordance with applicable equipment technical manuals, recovery technical manuals, winch operation procedures, and this SOP.


Recovery awareness training will ensure operators understand:

- Authorized operator-level self-recovery procedures

- Safe operation of assigned recovery systems and auxiliary equipment

- Recovery hazards and safety requirements

- Recovery limitations and prohibited actions

- Ground guide and communication requirements during recovery operations

- Conditions requiring recovery support from unit maintenance or higher-level recovery assets

- Procedures for reporting disabled equipment and requesting recovery assistance through the chain of command


Recovery awareness training will not authorize operators to conduct maintenance recovery operations beyond operator-level procedures authorized by technical manuals and command policy.


### 8.11 Safety Integration


Safety and risk management will be integrated into all driver training operations in accordance with AR 385-10, ATP 5-19, GTA 21-08-001, local installation policy, and this SOP.


Driver training operations will incorporate:

- Risk management

- Accident prevention

- Environmental considerations

- PPE requirements

- Ground guide enforcement

- Hazard identification

- Emergency procedures


Deliberate Risk Assessment Worksheets (DRAWs) will be completed for driver training operations as required by applicable regulations and command policy and approved by the appropriate level of command authority prior to execution.


Unsafe acts and unsafe conditions identified during training operations will be immediately corrected or reported through the chain of command.


### 8.12 Sustainment Training


Units will conduct sustainment training to maintain operator proficiency, PMCS standards, safety awareness, licensing compliance, and equipment familiarization in accordance with AR 600-55, applicable TC 21-305 series publications, TC 21-305-2 when applicable, applicable technical manuals, and this SOP.


Sustainment training will include periodic operator reevaluation, refresher training, PMCS reinforcement, safety training, and check rides appropriate to assigned equipment and mission requirements.


Sustainment training will address seasonal, environmental, regional, and terrain hazards affecting vehicle and equipment operations appropriate to assigned mission requirements and operating environments.


In accordance with AR 600-55, Army National Guard (ARNG) personnel will complete sustainment check rides every 2 years.


Sustainment training completion, reevaluations, and check rides will be documented within operator records and packets in accordance with AR 600-55.


### 8.13 Remedial Training


Remedial training will be conducted following accidents, unsafe acts, licensing violations, PMCS deficiencies, operator negligence, command-directed corrective actions, or demonstrated operator deficiencies.


Remedial training may require retraining, reevaluation, or requalification prior to restoration of operator privileges. 


The scope and severity of corrective actions will be determined by the chain of command based on the nature of the deficiency, unsafe act, accident, or violation.


Remedial training completion will be documented in operator records and packets.


### 8.14 Training Documentation


Training documentation will be maintained in accordance with AR 600-55, AR 25-400-2, DA PAM 750-8, section 10 of this SOP, and applicable CMDP requirements.


Training documentation will remain current, accurate, complete, standardized, and available for inspection.


### 8.15 Train-the-Trainer Requirements


Units assigned equipment requiring specialized operator training or qualification will develop and maintain internal instructor and examiner capability sufficient to support sustainment and mission requirements.


Units lacking qualified instructors, examiners, or trainers for assigned equipment will coordinate with supporting organizations, training support agencies, installation resources, or other qualified units to obtain required training support.


Units will conduct train-the-trainer operations as required to develop internal training capability and reduce reliance on external training support for assigned equipment and mission requirements.

## 11. SAFETY AND RISK MANAGEMENT

### 11.1 General


Safety and risk management are integral components of all operator training, licensing, sustainment, dispatch, maintenance, and vehicle operation activities conducted within the brigade.


All vehicle and equipment operations will be conducted in accordance with AR 385-10, ATP 5-19, applicable technical manuals, applicable training circulars, local installation safety requirements, and this SOP.


Commanders, leaders, instructors, examiners, supervisors, and operators are responsible for enforcing safety standards, identifying hazards, correcting unsafe conditions, and preventing accidents and injuries during all vehicle and equipment operations.


Safety compliance is subject to inspection and validation through CMDP, command inspections, safety inspections, and readiness evaluations.


### 11.2 Risk Management


Risk management will be integrated into all operator training, licensing, convoy, recovery, maintenance, dispatch, and operational activities in accordance with ATP 5-19 and AR 385-10.


Leaders will:

- Identify hazards

- Assess operational risk

- Develop and implement control measures

- Supervise operations

- Continuously assess hazards during operations


Deliberate Risk Assessment Worksheets (DRAWs) will be completed for training and operational activities as required by applicable regulations, installation policy, and command guidance.


DRAW approval authority will be determined by residual risk level and approved by the appropriate level of command authority prior to execution.


Risk management applies to:

- Driver training

- Convoy operations

- NVD operations

- Recovery operations

- PMCS operations

- Field operations

- Vehicle dispatch operations

- Trailer operations

- Hazardous operating conditions


### 11.3 Accident Prevention


Leaders at all levels will enforce accident prevention measures to reduce injuries, equipment damage, maintenance failures, and loss of operational capability.


Accident prevention measures will include:

- Enforcement of licensing requirements

- Enforcement of PMCS standards

- Ground guide enforcement

- Driver training and sustainment training

- Fatigue management

- Environmental hazard awareness

- Speed management

- Proper use of PPE

- Seatbelt enforcement

- Backing procedures

- Convoy discipline

- Risk management integration


Unsafe trends, repeat deficiencies, accidents, near misses, and operator negligence will be reviewed through command, maintenance, safety, and CMDP channels to identify corrective actions and prevent recurrence.


### 11.4 Unsafe Acts and Unsafe Conditions


Unsafe acts and unsafe conditions identified during training, maintenance, dispatch, or operational activities will be immediately corrected or reported through the chain of command.


Personnel will not:

- Operate unsafe equipment

- Ignore safety faults or deadlining conditions

- Falsify PMCS or maintenance documentation

- Bypass safety procedures or technical guidance

- Operate equipment outside the limits of qualification or authorization

- Continue operations when conditions exceed operator capability, equipment capability, or authorized risk levels


Leaders will immediately remove unsafe operators or unsafe equipment from operation until deficiencies are corrected.


### 11.5 Ground Guide Requirements


Ground guide procedures will be enforced in accordance with AR 385-10, TC 3-21.60, applicable technical manuals, and this SOP.


Ground guides are required during:

- Backing operations

- Limited visibility operations

- Congested areas

- Motor pool operations

- Recovery operations

- Trailer hookup operations

- Operations where visibility or maneuver space is restricted


Operators and ground guides will maintain continuous communication during movement operations requiring ground guides.


### 11.6 PPE Requirements


Personnel operating, training on, dispatching, servicing, guiding, recovering, loading, or maintaining Army vehicles and equipment will utilize required personal protective equipment (PPE) in accordance with AR 385-10, technical manuals, equipment publications, and local safety requirements.


Required PPE is dependent upon the equipment platform, operational environment, and mission being conducted and may include:

- ACH or approved helmet systems

- Eye protection

- Hearing protection

- Gloves

- Reflective equipment

- Protective footwear

- Protective masks or respiratory protection when required

- Crewman protective equipment when applicable


Leaders and supervisors will ensure personnel utilize PPE appropriate to the equipment platform, operational environment, mission, and hazards associated with the operation being conducted in accordance with AR 385-10, applicable technical manuals, convoy operations SOP, and local safety requirements.


Leaders and supervisors will ensure required PPE is available, serviceable, properly worn, and enforced during operations.


### 11.7 Environmental Considerations


Vehicle and equipment operations will comply with environmental protection requirements, hazardous material handling requirements, spill response procedures, and environmental reporting requirements in accordance with AR 200-1, applicable federal and state regulations, the brigade Environmental / HAZMAT SOP, unit SOPs, and local installation requirements.


Leaders and operators will immediately report spills, leaks, environmental hazards, and hazardous material incidents through the chain of command and respond in accordance with established environmental procedures.


### 11.8 Emergency Response Procedures


Units will establish emergency response procedures appropriate to assigned equipment, training areas, operational environments, and mission requirements in accordance with AR 385-10, applicable technical manuals, local installation requirements, and this SOP.


Emergency contact procedures, reporting requirements, evacuation procedures, and emergency response actions will be briefed prior to training and operational activities.

## 12. CMDP AND COMPLIANCE OVERSIGHT

### 12.1 General


The Command Maintenance Discipline Program (CMDP) establishes the brigade standard for operator licensing, driver training, PMCS execution, records management, dispatch operations, safety compliance, and maintenance discipline in accordance with AR 750-1, DA PAM 750-8, AR 600-55, and this SOP.


CMDP inspections, quality control (QC), quality assurance (QA), and command oversight procedures will be used to evaluate compliance, identify deficiencies, standardize operations, and improve operator readiness throughout the brigade.


CMDP oversight applies to:

- Operator licensing

- Driver training

- Sustainment training

- PMCS execution

- Records management

- Dispatch operations

- NVD qualification programs

- Safety compliance

- Operator packet management

- Maintenance documentation

- Corrective action tracking


### 12.2 CMDP Integration


CMDP requirements will be integrated into all operator licensing, training, maintenance, dispatch, and safety operations throughout the brigade.


Commanders and leaders at all levels will enforce CMDP standards and ensure deficiencies identified during inspections, audits, reviews, or assistance visits are corrected in a timely manner.


CMDP integration includes:

- Standardized operator packet management

- Licensing validation

- Sustainment tracking

- PMCS validation

- Maintenance documentation review

- Dispatch procedure validation

- Safety compliance inspections

- NVD program validation

- Corrective action tracking

- Readiness oversight


CMDP standards apply to all units operating Army vehicles and equipment regardless of organizational structure or maintenance support relationships.


### 12.3 Inspection Responsibilities


CMDP inspections and compliance reviews will be conducted at all levels using command-approved inspection checklists aligned with AR 600-55, AR 750-1, DA PAM 750-8, AR 385-10, applicable technical manuals, maintenance standards, higher headquarters guidance, and this SOP.


Inspection responsibilities include:

- Brigade-level oversight and trend analysis

- Battalion-level QA oversight and standardization

- Company-level QC validation and corrective action management

- Platoon and section-level packet validation and sustainment oversight


Inspections may include:

- Operator packet inspections

- Licensing validation

- Sustainment verification

- PMCS inspections

- Dispatch inspections

- Safety inspections

- Training documentation reviews

- NVD qualification validation

- Records audits

- Operator interviews

- Leader interviews


Inspection results and deficiencies will be documented and tracked through completion.


### 12.4 QC and QA Standards


Quality control (QC) and quality assurance (QA) procedures will be conducted to ensure operator licensing, training, maintenance, dispatch, safety, and records management operations comply with applicable regulations and brigade standards.


QC procedures are conducted at the unit level to identify and correct deficiencies during routine operations.


QA procedures are conducted at higher levels to validate standardization, compliance, and effectiveness of unit programs.


QC and QA operations will evaluate:

- Regulatory compliance

- Packet accuracy

- Sustainment compliance

- PMCS quality

- Maintenance documentation

- Fault documentation accuracy

- Readiness reporting accuracy

- Dispatch compliance

- Safety enforcement

- Training program effectiveness

- NVD sustainment compliance

- Corrective action effectiveness


CMDP inspections, command inspections, assistance visits, and maintenance reviews will incorporate QC and QA validation procedures.


### 12.5 Deficiency Tracking


Units will identify, document, track, and correct deficiencies identified during CMDP inspections, packet reviews, safety inspections, audits, assistance visits, or command reviews.


Deficiency tracking procedures will include:

- Identification of deficiencies

- Assignment of corrective actions

- Establishment of suspense dates

- Validation of corrective actions

- Trend analysis

- Root cause identification

- Escalation of unresolved deficiencies through the chain of command


Repeat deficiencies, systemic deficiencies, negligence, falsification, unsafe practices, and recurring compliance failures will be elevated through command and maintenance channels for corrective action.


### 12.6 Corrective Actions


Commanders and leaders will ensure deficiencies identified during inspections, reviews, audits, accidents, or compliance assessments are corrected promptly.


Corrective actions may include:

- Remedial training

- Sustainment training

- Reevaluation

- Packet corrections

- Licensing suspension

- License revocation

- PMCS retraining

- Safety counseling

- Increased supervision

- Command-directed corrective measures


Corrective actions will be documented and validated through QC, QA, CMDP, command review, follow-up inspection, packet review, or reinspection procedures.


### 12.7 Assistance Visits


Assistance visits may be conducted to assist units with operator licensing, training management, packet management, PMCS procedures, safety compliance, dispatch procedures, and CMDP requirements.


Assistance visits are intended to:

- Improve standardization

- Identify deficiencies

- Share best practices

- Assist units with corrective actions

- Improve regulatory compliance

- Improve readiness and operator proficiency


Assistance visits may incorporate MAIT/COMET principles intended to improve maintenance discipline, standardization, operator proficiency, and regulatory compliance through leader engagement and on-site instruction but do not replace formal inspections or command responsibilities.

Commanders, Master Drivers, and responsible leaders will review trends identified through:

- CMDP inspections

- Packet reviews

- Safety incidents

- Accident reporting

- Dispatch violations

- Sustainment deficiencies

- PMCS deficiencies

- Licensing violations

- NVD deficiencies

- Repeat inspection findings


Trend analysis will be used to:

- Improve maintenance discipline

- Reduce accidents and unsafe practices

- Improve readiness


### 12.9 Program Evaluation


Commanders and Master Drivers will periodically evaluate unit operator training and licensing programs to ensure compliance, effectiveness, readiness, and standardization.


Program evaluations will assess:

- Regulatory compliance

- Packet management

- PMCS proficiency

- Safety compliance

- Dispatch compliance

- NVD program effectiveness

- QC and QA effectiveness

- Corrective action management


Program evaluations will be used to improve operator readiness, maintenance discipline, safety compliance, and CMDP effectiveness throughout the brigade.


### 12.10 Compliance Enforcement


Personnel failing to comply with applicable regulations, safety requirements, licensing requirements, maintenance standards, CMDP requirements, or this SOP may be subject to:

- Remedial training

- Reevaluation

- Increased supervision

- Suspension of operator privileges

- Revocation of operator privileges

- Administrative action

- Corrective action through the chain of command


Commanders retain authority to suspend or revoke operator privileges when required to protect personnel, equipment, safety, or mission readiness.

## 13. SUMMARY OF ADMINISTRATIVE REVISION

This administrative revision, dated 14 May 2026:

- Establishes brigade-wide standards for driver and operator training, testing, qualification, licensing, and sustainment operations in accordance with AR 600-55.
- Establishes requirements for learner permits, supervised operator training, sustainment training, remedial training, and annual operator evaluation requirements.
- Clarifies commander responsibilities for Driver and Operator Standardization Program management, operator supervision, and licensing oversight.
- Establishes minimum standards for PMCS integration into operator training and licensing validation.
- Establishes requirements for Master Drivers, License Instructors, License Examiners, and operator training documentation.
- Expands requirements for vehicle commander, senior occupant, co-driver, and assistant driver responsibilities.
- Establishes requirements for Night Vision Device (NVD) qualification, sustainment training, and annual recertification.
- Establishes operator records management requirements, including driver packets, DA Form 348 maintenance, training rosters, licensing documentation, and sustainment records.
- Establishes sustainment and retraining requirements following accidents, unsafe operation, licensing violations, or demonstrated operator deficiencies.
- Aligns operator training and licensing procedures with Command Maintenance Discipline Program (CMDP) inspection standards and maintenance readiness requirements.
- Consolidates operator training, licensing, PMCS instruction, and driver sustainment requirements into the Driver Training Standard Operating Procedure (CMDP Reference 10(10)-1).
