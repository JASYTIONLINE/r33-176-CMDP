1. PURPOSE

This SOP establishes procedures for vehicle and equipment dispatch operations within [UNIT NAME] in accordance with AR 58-1, AR 600-55, and DA Pam 750-1.

2. APPLICABILITY

This SOP applies to all personnel assigned or attached to [UNIT NAME] who dispatch, operate, or manage vehicles and equipment.

3. REFERENCES

  • AR 58-1, Management, Acquisition, and Use of Motor Vehicles
  • AR 600-55, The Army Driver and Operator Standardization Program
  • DA Pam 750-1, Commanders' Maintenance Handbook
  • Unit SOPs and policies

4. RESPONSIBILITIES

4.1 Commander

  • Establishes dispatch procedures
  • Designates dispatch authority
  • Reviews dispatch operations

4.2 Dispatcher

  • Processes dispatch requests
  • Verifies operator licensing
  • Maintains dispatch records

4.3 Operators

  • Complete PMCS before dispatch
  • Operate vehicles safely
  • Complete dispatch procedures

5. PROCEDURES

5.1 Dispatch Authority

a. Commander designates dispatch authority in writing

b. Dispatch authority delegated to: [Designated Personnel/Positions]

c. After-hours dispatch authority: [Designated Personnel]

d. Emergency dispatch procedures established

5.2 Pre-Dispatch Requirements

a. Operator Verification:

  • Valid military/government operator license
  • Licensed on specific vehicle/equipment
  • License not suspended or revoked
  • Medically qualified to operate

b. Vehicle Requirements:

  • PMCS completed and documented
  • No deadlining faults
  • Required safety equipment present
  • Current dispatched mileage/hours documented

c. Mission Requirements:

  • Valid mission/trip ticket
  • Approved by dispatch authority
  • Route/destination identified
  • Expected return time documented

5.3 Dispatch Procedures

a. Operator requests dispatch from dispatcher

b. Dispatcher verifies license and vehicle status

c. Operator performs and documents PMCS

d. Dispatcher issues dispatch (DA Form 5987-E or GCSS-Army)

e. Document beginning mileage/hours

f. Operator signs for vehicle

5.4 During Operations

a. Operate vehicle IAW regulations and safety requirements

b. Conduct during-operations PMCS

c. Report accidents/incidents immediately

d. Document fuel consumption

e. Do not deviate from authorized route without approval

5.5 Turn-In Procedures

a. Complete after-operations PMCS

b. Report any deficiencies discovered

c. Document ending mileage/hours

d. Fuel vehicle if required

e. Close dispatch in GCSS-Army or on trip ticket

f. Return keys to dispatcher

5.6 Dispatch Records

a. Documentation Required:

  • DA Form 5987-E (Motor Equipment Dispatch) or GCSS-Army
  • DA Form 5988-E (Equipment Maintenance and Inspection Worksheet)
  • Accident/incident reports if applicable

b. Record Retention:

  • Maintain dispatch records IAW AR 25-400-2
  • File completed dispatches by vehicle
  • Retain for [specified period per records schedule]

5.7 After-Hours/Weekend Dispatch

a. Obtain approval from designated authority

b. Document emergency/after-hours dispatch

c. Complete all dispatch requirements

d. Close dispatch on next duty day

6. DISPATCH LOOP CLOSURE

a. Close all dispatches within 24 hours of mission completion

b. Review open dispatches daily

c. Report overdue vehicles immediately

d. Document loop closure in dispatch log

7. RECORDS AND REPORTS

  • DA Form 5987-E (Motor Equipment Dispatch)
  • DA Form 5988-E (Equipment Maintenance and Inspection Worksheet)
  • Dispatch log
  • Mileage/hours tracking
  • Fuel consumption records

8. SAFETY

  • Operators will not operate while fatigued
  • Seat belts required for all occupants
  • Cell phone use while operating prohibited
  • Report unsafe conditions immediately
  • Defensive driving techniques required

9. ACCIDENT/INCIDENT REPORTING

a. Stop and render aid if safe

b. Notify dispatcher and chain of command immediately

c. Obtain required information from other parties

d. Complete SF 91 (Motor Vehicle Accident Report)

e. Do not admit fault

f. Request law enforcement response as required
