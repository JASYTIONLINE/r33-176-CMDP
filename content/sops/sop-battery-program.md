## 1. PURPOSE

This SOP establishes procedures for battery maintenance, testing, storage, and disposal within [UNIT NAME] in accordance with TB 43-0134 and DA Pam 750-1.

---

## 2. APPLICABILITY

This SOP applies to all personnel assigned or attached to [UNIT NAME] who handle, test, charge, store, or turn in batteries.

---

## 3. REFERENCES

- TB 43-0134, Battery Disposition and Disposal
- DA Pam 750-1, Commanders' Maintenance Handbook
- TM 9-6140-200-14, Lead-Acid Storage Batteries
- Installation HAZMAT/Environmental SOP

---

## 4. RESPONSIBILITIES

### 4.1 Commander
- Establishes battery maintenance program
- Appoints Battery Maintenance Operator in writing
- Ensures resources for battery maintenance

### 4.2 Battery Maintenance Operator
- Manages battery testing and maintenance
- Maintains battery records
- Coordinates battery turn-in

### 4.3 Operators
- Report battery deficiencies
- Perform operator-level battery care
- Use proper PPE when handling batteries

---

## 5. PROCEDURES

### 5.1 Battery Testing
a. Test batteries IAW TB 43-0134 using approved equipment  
b. Test batteries at least quarterly or when deficiency suspected  
c. Document test results  
d. Condemn batteries that fail testing criteria

### 5.2 Battery Charging
a. **Location:**
   - Charge batteries only in designated, ventilated areas
   - Ensure adequate ventilation to prevent hydrogen buildup
   - No smoking or open flames within 50 feet

b. **Procedures:**
   - Inspect battery before charging
   - Verify electrolyte level before charging
   - Connect charger properly (positive to positive)
   - Monitor charging progress
   - Allow battery to cool before installation

### 5.3 Battery Storage
a. **Serviceable Batteries:**
   - Store in cool, dry, ventilated area
   - Store on wooden pallets or approved racks (not concrete)
   - Maintain batteries in charged condition
   - Inspect stored batteries monthly

b. **Unserviceable Batteries:**
   - Store separately from serviceable batteries
   - Mark clearly as "UNSERVICEABLE"
   - Prepare for turn-in promptly
   - Store upright to prevent leakage

### 5.4 Battery Handling Safety
a. **Required PPE:**
   - Acid-resistant apron
   - Face shield or safety goggles
   - Acid-resistant gloves
   - Safety shoes

b. **Precautions:**
   - Never smoke near batteries
   - Remove jewelry before handling
   - Lift batteries properly using handles/carriers
   - Never place tools or metal objects on batteries

### 5.5 Battery Turn-In
a. Complete turn-in documentation  
b. Coordinate with supply for DRMO turn-in  
c. Transport batteries upright and secured  
d. Maintain turn-in records

### 5.6 Spill Response
a. Neutralize acid spills with baking soda  
b. Wear required PPE during cleanup  
c. Dispose of cleanup materials as HAZMAT  
d. Report spills IAW Environmental SOP

---

## 6. BATTERY MAINTENANCE AREA REQUIREMENTS

- Emergency eyewash station within 25 feet
- Spill kit with neutralizing agent
- Fire extinguisher (Class B/C)
- Adequate ventilation
- Posted safety procedures
- Required PPE available
- Battery testing equipment

---

## 7. RECORDS AND REPORTS

- Battery test records
- Turn-in documentation
- Inspection checklists
- Training records

---

## 8. TRAINING

Battery Maintenance Operators will receive:
- TB 43-0134 familiarization
- Battery testing equipment operation
- Safety and PPE requirements
- Spill response procedures
