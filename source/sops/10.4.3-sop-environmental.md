Summary: This SOP establishes standards, policies, and procedures governing hazardous material operations, environmental protection, hazardous waste management, spill prevention, spill response, hazardous material transportation, ammunition and explosives environmental safety, and environmental compliance operations within the 176th EN BDE. It provides guidance in accordance with AR 200-1, AR 350-1, AR 385-10, AR 600-55, AR 750-1, DA PAM 750-8, DA PAM 385-64, OSHA Hazard Communication standards, federal hazardous material transportation regulations, Texas environmental reporting requirements, EPA spill reporting guidance, and applicable technical manuals. When used in conjunction with applicable regulations, installation environmental guidance, and technical manuals, this SOP establishes the environmental protection and hazardous material management program for the 176th EN BDE.

Commanders Review: Environmental Protection Responsibilities Chapter 4; Hazardous Material Handling Chapter 5; Hazardous Material Storage Chapter 6; Hazardous Material Transportation Chapter 7; Ammunition and Explosives Chapter 8; Spill Prevention and Response Chapter 9; Hazardous Waste Management Chapter 10; Training Chapter 11; Inspections Chapter 12; Incident Reporting Chapter 13; Safety Chapter 14

## 1. PURPOSE

This SOP establishes procedures for hazardous material operations and environmental protection within 176th Engineer Brigade (TXARNG) in accordance with AR 200-1, AR 350-1, AR 385-10, AR 600-55, AR 750-1, DA PAM 750-8, DA PAM 385-64, applicable technical manuals, OSHA standards, federal transportation regulations, and Texas environmental reporting requirements.

This SOP establishes Brigade-level guidance for hazardous material handling, environmental protection, hazardous waste management, spill prevention, spill response, ammunition and explosives environmental considerations, and hazardous material transportation operations.

- Monitors hazardous material storage and hazardous waste accumulation areas

- Report spills, leaks, and unsafe conditions immediately
- Follow applicable SDS guidance and technical manuals

## 5. HAZARDOUS MATERIAL HANDLING

### 5.1 General Handling Procedures
a. Personnel will review applicable SDS guidance before handling unfamiliar hazardous materials  
b. Hazardous materials will remain properly labeled at all times  
c. Personnel will not mix chemicals unless authorized by SDS guidance or technical manuals  
d. Personnel will use required PPE during hazardous material operations  
e. Eating, drinking, smoking, or vaping is prohibited in hazardous material handling areas

### 5.2 Fuel Handling Procedures
a. Fuel operations will occur only in approved areas  
b. Fire extinguishers and spill kits will remain present during fueling operations  
c. Smoking and open flames are prohibited within 50 feet of fueling operations  
d. Fuel spills will be reported immediately  
e. Personnel will not leave fueling operations unattended

### 5.3 Battery Handling Procedures
a. Personnel handling batteries will wear eye protection and gloves  
b. Damaged or leaking batteries will be isolated immediately  
c. Battery charging areas will remain ventilated  
d. Battery acid spills will be neutralized and reported immediately

### 5.4 Bloodborne Pathogen Procedures
a. Personnel exposed to blood or bodily fluids will notify supervision immediately  
b. Bloodborne pathogen cleanup kits will remain available in designated areas  
c. Personnel conducting cleanup operations will use protective gloves and equipment  
d. Contaminated materials will be disposed of in accordance with medical waste guidance

---

## 6. HAZARDOUS MATERIAL STORAGE

### 6.1 Storage Requirements
a. Hazardous materials will only be stored within approved storage areas  
b. Hazardous materials will remain in approved containers  
c. Hazardous material containers will remain labeled and secured  
d. Secondary containment will be used when required  
e. Incompatible hazardous materials will not be stored together

### 6.2 Flammable Material Storage
a. Flammable materials will remain separated from ignition sources  
b. Approved flammable storage cabinets will be used when required  
c. NO SMOKING signs will remain posted within flammable storage areas  
d. Fire extinguishers will remain accessible within storage areas

### 6.3 Hazardous Waste Storage
a. Hazardous waste accumulation points will remain properly labeled  
b. Hazardous waste containers will remain closed when not actively used  
c. Contaminated absorbents and dry sweep will be handled as hazardous waste when applicable  
d. Units will coordinate hazardous waste turn-in through approved procedures

---

## 7. HAZARDOUS MATERIAL TRANSPORTATION

### 7.1 Transportation Requirements
a. Drivers transporting hazardous materials will meet AR 600-55 licensing requirements  
b. Vehicles transporting hazardous materials will maintain spill kits and fire extinguishers  
c. Hazardous materials will be properly secured during transportation  
d. Damaged or leaking containers will not be transported

### 7.2 Convoy Operations
a. Convoy commanders will inspect hazardous cargo prior to movement  
b. Hazardous material spills during convoy operations will be reported immediately  
c. Drivers will stop operations immediately when hazardous conditions exist

### 7.3 Accident Procedures
a. Personnel will isolate hazardous spill areas during transportation accidents  
b. Personnel will notify emergency services and supervisory personnel immediately  
c. Spill containment materials will be deployed when safe to do so

---

## 8. AMMUNITION AND EXPLOSIVES

### 8.1 Ammunition Safety
a. Ammunition operations will comply with DA PAM 385-64  
b. Smoking and open flames are prohibited near ammunition operations  
c. Damaged ammunition will be isolated immediately  
d. Ammunition storage areas will remain clean and free of combustible debris

### 8.2 Ammunition Transportation
a. Ammunition will remain properly secured during transportation  
b. Vehicles transporting ammunition will maintain fire extinguishers  
c. Ammunition incidents will be reported immediately through command channels

---

## 9. SPILL PREVENTION AND RESPONSE

### 9.1 Spill Prevention
a. Drip pans will be placed beneath leaking equipment  
b. Spill kits will remain available during hazardous material operations  
c. Personnel will inspect fuel hoses, valves, and fittings prior to operations  
d. Leaking containers will be removed from service immediately

### 9.2 Spill Response
a. Personnel will stop spill sources when safe to do so  
b. Personnel will isolate spill areas immediately  
c. Spill response materials will be deployed to prevent contamination spread  
d. Personnel will notify supervisory personnel and environmental authorities immediately

### 9.3 Environmental Reporting
a. POL spills meeting Texas reporting thresholds will be reported immediately  
b. Spills impacting waterways or storm drains will be reported immediately  
c. Environmental contamination incidents will be documented and investigated

---

## 10. HAZARDOUS WASTE MANAGEMENT

### 10.1 Hazardous Waste Procedures
a. Hazardous waste will remain segregated and properly labeled  
b. Personnel will not place hazardous waste into standard trash containers  
c. Used oil, solvents, contaminated absorbents, batteries, aerosol cans, and contaminated soil will be processed through approved disposal procedures  
d. Hazardous waste accumulation areas will remain organized and inspected

### 10.2 Environmental Protection Procedures
a. Personnel will prevent hazardous materials from entering storm drains or waterways  
b. Wash rack operations will only occur within approved areas  
c. Personnel will not intentionally discharge hazardous materials onto the ground  
d. Units will police hazardous waste and contamination from training areas

---

## 11. TRAINING

All personnel will receive training on:
- Hazardous material handling procedures
- Environmental protection procedures
- Spill prevention and spill response procedures
- Hazardous waste handling procedures
- PPE requirements
- SDS usage procedures
- Transportation procedures
- Ammunition and explosives environmental requirements
- Emergency reporting procedures

---

## 12. INSPECTIONS

### 12.1 Environmental Inspections
a. Leaders will inspect hazardous material storage areas routinely  
b. Spill kits and fire extinguishers will be inspected regularly  
c. Hazardous waste accumulation areas will remain inspected and documented  
d. Deficiencies will be corrected immediately or elevated through command channels

### 12.2 CMDP Integration
a. Environmental protection procedures will be incorporated into CMDP inspections  
b. Hazardous material operations will remain subject to command inspection programs  
c. Inspection findings will be tracked through corrective action procedures

---

## 13. INCIDENT REPORTING

### 13.1 General Reporting Requirements
a. Personnel will immediately report hazardous spills, leaks, contamination, and unsafe conditions  
b. Supervisors will notify command channels and environmental personnel when required  
c. Environmental incidents will be documented in accordance with installation guidance

### 13.2 Emergency Reporting
a. Spills impacting waterways or storm drains will be reported immediately  
b. Fuel spills meeting Texas reporting thresholds will be reported immediately  
c. Ammunition incidents and hazardous exposures will be reported immediately

---

## 14. SAFETY

- Personnel will use required PPE during hazardous material operations
- Unsafe hazardous material operations will be stopped immediately
- Personnel will report unsafe conditions immediately
- Environmental contamination hazards will be corrected immediately
- Personnel will follow SDS guidance and technical manual safety procedures
- Fire extinguishers and spill kits will remain accessible during hazardous material operations
