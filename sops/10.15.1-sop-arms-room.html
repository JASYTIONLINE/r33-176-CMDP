1. PURPOSE

This SOP establishes procedures for arms room operations and weapons maintenance within [UNIT NAME] in accordance with AR 190-11, AR 710-2, and DA Pam 750-1.

2. APPLICABILITY

This SOP applies to all personnel assigned or attached to [UNIT NAME] who access the arms room, issue/receive weapons, or perform weapons maintenance.

3. REFERENCES

  • AR 190-11, Physical Security of Arms, Ammunition, and Explosives
  • AR 710-2, Supply Policy Below the National Level
  • DA Pam 750-1, Commanders' Maintenance Handbook
  • Applicable Weapons Technical Manuals (-10 and -23&P series)

4. RESPONSIBILITIES

4.1 Commander

  • Responsible for arms room security and accountability
  • Appoints Armorer, Assistant Armorers, Arms Room Officer in writing
  • Conducts/reviews weapons inventories IAW AR 190-11

4.2 Arms Room Officer

  • Provides officer oversight of arms room operations
  • Conducts monthly inspections
  • Reviews arms room documentation

4.3 Unit Armorer

  • Maintains weapons accountability
  • Performs weapons maintenance
  • Manages arms room operations

4.4 Individual Soldiers

  • Responsible for weapons issued to them
  • Perform operator-level maintenance
  • Return weapons in serviceable condition

5. PROCEDURES

5.1 Arms Room Access

a. Only authorized personnel may enter the arms room

b. Arms room access roster posted and current

c. Two-person integrity maintained when possible

d. Visitors logged in arms room visitor log

e. Arms room secured when unattended

5.2 Weapons Issue

a. Verify individual on hand receipt or authorization

b. Conduct joint inventory with receiver

c. Verify weapon serial number

d. Document issue in arms room log

e. Issue required accessories (magazines, sling, etc.)

5.3 Weapons Turn-In

a. Clear all weapons before entering arms room

b. Armorer inspects weapon for serviceability

c. Conduct joint inventory with individual

d. Document turn-in in arms room log

e. Report deficiencies immediately

5.4 Weapons Inventories

a. Sensitive Items Inventory:

  • Conduct monthly serial number inventory
  • Two personnel conduct inventory
  • Document on DA Form 2062 or hand receipt

b. Physical Security Inspections:

  • Conducted IAW AR 190-11
  • Documented and filed

5.5 Weapons Maintenance

a. Operator Maintenance:

  • Clean weapons after each use
  • Perform PMCS IAW TM
  • Report deficiencies to armorer

b. Armorer Maintenance:

  • Inspect weapons during turn-in
  • Perform organizational maintenance
  • Document maintenance actions
  • Submit work orders for repairs beyond capability

c. Weapons Gauging:

  • Conducted IAW applicable TM
  • Documented on DA Form 2404 or GCSS-Army
  • Report failures immediately

5.6 Weapons Cleaning

a. Clean weapons in designated area only

b. Use only approved cleaning materials

c. Follow TM cleaning procedures

d. Armorer inspects cleaned weapons before storage

6. SECURITY REQUIREMENTS

  • Arms room constructed IAW AR 190-11
  • IDS (Intrusion Detection System) operational
  • Key control IAW AR 190-11
  • Emergency procedures posted
  • Two-person integrity rule enforced

7. RECORDS AND REPORTS

  • DA Form 2062 (Hand Receipt)
  • Arms room log (issue/turn-in)
  • Inventory documentation
  • Maintenance records
  • Key control records
  • Visitor log

8. TRAINING

Armorer personnel will receive:

  • Unit Armorer Course or equivalent
  • Weapons maintenance training
  • Physical security training
  • GCSS-Army armory transactions

9. SAFETY

  • All weapons cleared before entering arms room
  • No live ammunition in arms room during maintenance
  • Proper ventilation during cleaning operations
  • PPE as required by cleaning materials
