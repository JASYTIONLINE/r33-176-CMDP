1. PURPOSE

This SOP establishes procedures for the execution of maintenance operations within [UNIT NAME] in accordance with AR 750-1, DA Pam 750-1, and applicable Technical Manuals.

2. APPLICABILITY

This SOP applies to all personnel assigned or attached to [UNIT NAME] who perform, supervise, or manage maintenance operations.

3. REFERENCES

  • AR 750-1, The Army Maintenance Management System (TAMMS)
  • DA Pam 750-1, Commanders' Maintenance Handbook
  • DA Pam 750-8, The Army Maintenance Management System (TAMMS) Users Manual
  • Applicable Technical Manuals (TMs) for assigned equipment

4. RESPONSIBILITIES

4.1 Commander

  • Ensures maintenance program compliance with regulatory requirements
  • Appoints maintenance personnel in writing
  • Reviews maintenance status at least monthly

4.2 Maintenance Officer

  • Supervises all maintenance operations
  • Ensures maintenance procedures comply with regulations
  • Reviews and validates maintenance requests

4.3 Motor Sergeant / Maintenance NCO

  • Supervises daily maintenance operations
  • Ensures equipment is maintained to standard
  • Manages maintenance personnel and work assignments

4.4 Operators

  • Perform PMCS before, during, and after equipment operation
  • Report deficiencies to supervisor immediately
  • Maintain operator-level equipment records

5. PROCEDURES

5.1 Preventive Maintenance Checks and Services (PMCS)

a. Operators will perform PMCS IAW applicable TM -10 and -20 series manuals

b. Before operations: Complete before PMCS and document faults

c. During operations: Monitor equipment for abnormalities

d. After operations: Complete after PMCS and document findings

e. Weekly/Monthly: Complete scheduled services as required

5.2 Fault Reporting

a. Operators report faults immediately to supervisor

b. Supervisor evaluates fault and determines repair level

c. Document faults on DA Form 5988-E or in GCSS-Army

d. Non-mission capable faults reported to commander within 24 hours

5.3 Maintenance Requests

a. Submit maintenance requests through GCSS-Army

b. Include accurate fault description and symptoms

c. Supervisor validates and prioritizes requests

d. Track work orders to completion

5.4 Scheduled Services

a. Conduct scheduled services IAW TM requirements

b. Document services in GCSS-Army

c. Order parts in advance of scheduled services

d. Coordinate shop time for major services

6. RECORDS AND REPORTS

  • DA Form 5988-E (Equipment Maintenance and Inspection Worksheet)
  • GCSS-Army maintenance records
  • DA Form 2408 series as required
  • Monthly maintenance status reports

7. TRAINING

All personnel will receive training on:

  • PMCS procedures for assigned equipment
  • GCSS-Army maintenance transactions
  • Fault reporting procedures
  • Safety requirements for maintenance operations

8. SAFETY

  • Use required PPE during all maintenance operations
  • Follow TM safety precautions
  • Report unsafe conditions immediately
  • Lock out/tag out procedures for equipment under repair
