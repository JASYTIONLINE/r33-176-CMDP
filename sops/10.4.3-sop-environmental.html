1. PURPOSE

This SOP establishes procedures for environmental compliance and hazardous material (HAZMAT) management within [UNIT NAME] in accordance with AR 200-1 and applicable federal, state, and local regulations.

2. APPLICABILITY

This SOP applies to all personnel assigned or attached to [UNIT NAME] who handle, store, use, or dispose of hazardous materials or generate hazardous waste.

3. REFERENCES

  • AR 200-1, Environmental Protection and Enhancement
  • DA Pam 750-1, Commanders' Maintenance Handbook
  • 40 CFR (Code of Federal Regulations) - Environmental Protection
  • Installation Environmental SOP
  • Safety Data Sheets (SDS) for unit HAZMAT

4. RESPONSIBILITIES

4.1 Commander

  • Ensures environmental compliance program
  • Appoints Environmental Officer in writing
  • Provides resources for environmental compliance

4.2 Environmental Officer

  • Manages unit environmental program
  • Conducts environmental inspections
  • Coordinates with installation environmental office

4.3 All Personnel

  • Follow environmental procedures
  • Report spills and releases immediately
  • Use proper PPE when handling HAZMAT

5. PROCEDURES

5.1 Hazardous Material Management

a. Procurement:

  • Obtain HAZMAT through approved channels only
  • Maintain minimum quantities necessary
  • Verify SDS is available for all HAZMAT

b. Storage:

  • Store HAZMAT in approved containers
  • Segregate incompatible materials
  • Maintain secondary containment as required
  • Label all containers IAW regulations
  • HAZMAT storage areas will be inspected weekly

c. Use:

  • Use HAZMAT only for intended purpose
  • Follow SDS handling procedures
  • Use required PPE
  • Minimize waste generation

5.2 Hazardous Waste Management

a. Identification:

  • Identify all waste streams generated
  • Determine if waste is hazardous
  • Label containers as "Hazardous Waste"

b. Accumulation:

  • Use satellite accumulation areas as authorized
  • Do not exceed 55-gallon limit at satellite areas
  • Date containers when accumulation begins

c. Disposal:

  • Coordinate disposal through installation DRMO/Environmental
  • Never dispose of HAZMAT in drains or trash
  • Maintain disposal records

5.3 Spill Prevention and Response

a. Prevention:

  • Use drip pans during fluid operations
  • Inspect containers for leaks
  • Maintain secondary containment

b. Response:

  • Stop the source if safe to do so
  • Contain the spill using available materials
  • Report to supervisor immediately
  • Complete spill report documentation
  • Clean up using approved methods

c. Spill Kits:

  • Maintain spill kits in HAZMAT storage areas
  • Inspect spill kits monthly
  • Replenish kits after use

5.4 Used Oil Management

a. Store used oil in approved containers

b. Label containers as "Used Oil"

c. Do not mix used oil with other materials

d. Coordinate recycling/disposal through installation

5.5 Antifreeze Management

a. Store used antifreeze separately

b. Use approved containers

c. Coordinate recycling through installation

6. RECORDS AND REPORTS

  • HAZMAT inventory
  • SDS files (physical or electronic access)
  • Spill reports
  • Inspection checklists
  • Training records

7. TRAINING

All personnel handling HAZMAT will receive:

  • Initial HAZMAT awareness training
  • Annual refresher training
  • Spill response training
  • SDS familiarization

8. SAFETY

  • Required PPE for HAZMAT handling:
  • Chemical-resistant gloves
  • Safety glasses/goggles
  • Apron as needed
  • Face shield for splash hazards
  • Emergency eyewash within 25 feet of HAZMAT use areas
  • Fire extinguisher available in HAZMAT storage areas
