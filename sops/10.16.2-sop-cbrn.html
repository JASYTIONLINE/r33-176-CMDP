1. PURPOSE

This SOP establishes procedures for CBRN (Chemical, Biological, Radiological, Nuclear) equipment maintenance, training, and operations within [UNIT NAME] in accordance with AR 350-1, FM 3-11, and DA Pam 750-1.

2. APPLICABILITY

This SOP applies to all personnel assigned or attached to [UNIT NAME] who are issued CBRN equipment or participate in CBRN operations.

3. REFERENCES

  • AR 350-1, Army Training and Leader Development
  • FM 3-11, CBRN Operations
  • DA Pam 750-1, Commanders' Maintenance Handbook
  • TM 3-4240-series (Protective Mask TMs)
  • TB 43-0116, CBRN Equipment Maintenance Guidance

4. RESPONSIBILITIES

4.1 Commander

  • Ensures CBRN readiness program
  • Appoints CBRN personnel in writing
  • Provides resources for CBRN training

4.2 CBRN Officer

  • Provides officer oversight of CBRN program
  • Reviews CBRN status and readiness
  • Coordinates CBRN requirements

4.3 CBRN Primary

  • Manages unit CBRN program
  • Conducts equipment maintenance
  • Coordinates CBRN training

4.4 Individual Soldiers

  • Maintain issued CBRN equipment
  • Complete required CBRN training
  • Report equipment deficiencies

5. PROCEDURES

5.1 CBRN Equipment Issue

a. Issue protective masks by proper size fitting

b. Conduct mask fit test

c. Document issue on hand receipt

d. Issue required accessories and carrier

e. Provide operator training on issued equipment

5.2 CBRN Equipment Maintenance

a. Operator Maintenance:

  • Clean mask after each use
  • Inspect mask components weekly
  • Store mask properly when not in use
  • Report deficiencies to CBRN NCO

b. Organizational Maintenance:

  • Conduct quarterly serviceability inspections
  • Replace filters IAW TM/TB guidance
  • Document maintenance in GCSS-Army
  • Submit work orders for repairs

c. Annual Service:

  • Complete annual fit test
  • Replace consumable components as required
  • Document annual service

5.3 CBRN Equipment Accountability

a. Maintain current hand receipts

b. Conduct monthly inventories of CBRN equipment

c. Report losses/damages immediately

d. Coordinate turn-in of excess/unserviceable equipment

5.4 CBRN Training

a. Individual Training:

  • Mask confidence training annually
  • MOPP gear donning/doffing
  • Decontamination procedures
  • CBRN awareness

b. Collective Training:

  • Integrate CBRN into unit training
  • Conduct CBRN lanes during field exercises
  • Practice reconnaissance and survey procedures

c. Certification:

  • Maintain current certifications for CBRN personnel
  • Document training in DTMS

5.5 CBRN Room/Storage

a. Store CBRN equipment in clean, dry location

b. Protect from sunlight and extreme temperatures

c. Segregate serviceable from unserviceable equipment

d. Maintain inventory of CBRN supplies

6. RECORDS AND REPORTS

  • DA Form 2062 (Hand Receipt)
  • GCSS-Army maintenance records
  • Training records in DTMS
  • Fit test documentation
  • Inspection checklists

7. TRAINING DOCUMENTATION

  • Individual mask fit tests
  • CBRN training attendance
  • Certification records
  • DTMS entries

8. SAFETY

  • Never use damaged protective equipment
  • Ensure adequate ventilation during training
  • Medical screening before confidence training
  • Follow TM safety precautions
