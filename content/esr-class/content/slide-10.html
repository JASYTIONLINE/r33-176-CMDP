Single Point of Failure

Common ESR issues include work orders that are not updated, equipment waiting on action even when parts are available, and faults that remain unaddressed. These patterns often point to follow-through problems rather than a failure of the system itself.

This slide reinforces the idea that the squeaky wheel gets attention. Leaders must review the ESR regularly, identify the oldest or most important faults, communicate priorities to support elements, and follow up until equipment moves through the system to completion.
